PARABOLA

Chord joining two points

The equation of a chord of the parabola \(y^2\) = 4ax joining its two points P(\(t_1\)) and Q(\(t_2\)) is
y(\(t_1 + t_2\)) = 2x + 2a\(t_1t_2\)

NOTE :

(i) If PQ is focal chord then \(t_1t_2\) = -1

(i) Extremities of focal chord can be taken at (\(at^2\), 2at) & (\(a\over t^2\), \(-2a\over t\))


Line & A Parabola

(a) The line y = mx + c meets the parabola \(y^2\) = 4ax in two points real, coincident or imaginary according as a >=< cm \(\implies\) condition of tangency is, c = \(a\over m\)

Note : Line y = mx + c will be tangent to parabola \(x^2\) = 4ay if c = -a\(m^2\)

(b) Length of the chord intercepted by the parabola \(y^2\) = 4ax on the line y = mx + c is \(4\over m^2\)\(\sqrt{a(1+m^2)(a-mc)}\)

Note : Length of the focal chord making an angle \(\alpha\) with the x-axis is 4a \({cosec}^2\alpha\)

Tangent to the parabola \(y^2\) = 4ax

(a)   Point form

Equation of tangent to the given parabola at its point (\(x_1, y_1\)) is y\(y_1\) = 2a(x + \(x_1\))

(b)   Slope form

Equation of tangent to the given parabola whose slope is 'm', is y = mx + \(a\over m\), (m \(\ne\) 0)

point of contact is (\(a\over m^2\), \(2a\over m\))

(c)   Parametric form

Equation of tangent to the given parabola at its point P(t), is ty = x + a\(t^2\)

Note : Point of intersection of the tangents at the points \(t_1\) & \(t_2\) is [a\(t_1t_2\), a(\(t_1 + t_2\))].


Normal to the parabola \(y^2\) = 4ax

(a)   Point form

Equation of tangent to the given parabola at its point (\(x_1, y_1\)) is y - \(y_1\) = \(-y_1\over 2a\)(x - \(x_1\))

(b)   Slope form

Equation of tangent to the given parabola whose slope is 'm', is y = mx - 2am - a\(m^3\)

foot of the normal is (\(am^2\), -2am)

(c)   Parametric form

Equation of tangent to the given parabola at its point P(t), is y + tx = 2at + a\(t^3\)

Note : Point of intersection of the normals at the points \(t_1\) & \(t_2\) is [(a\({t_1}^2 + {t_2}^2 + t_1t_2 + 2\)), -a\(t_1t_2(t_1 + t_2)\)].

NOTE :

If the family of straight lines can be represented by an equation \({\lambda}^2\)P + \(\lambda\)Q + R = 0 where \(\lambda\) is a parameter and P, Q, R are linear functions of x and y then the family of lines will be tangent to the curve \(Q^2\) = 4PR.


Pair of tangents

The equation of the pair of tangents which can be drawn from any point P(\(x_1\),\(y_1\)) outside the parabola to the parabola \(y^2\) = 4ax is given by : S\(S_1\) = \(T_2\) where :

S = \(y^2\) - 4ax ; \(S_1\) = \({y_1}^2\) - 4a\(x_1\) ; T = y\(y_1\) = 2a(x + \(x_1\)).


Director circle

Locus of the point of intersection of the perpendicular tangents to the parabola \(y^2\) = 4ax is called the director circle. It's equation is x + a = 0 which is parabola's own directrix.


Chord of Contact

Equation of the chord of contact of tangents drawn from a point P(\(x_1, y_1\)) is y\(y_1\) = 2a(x + \(x_1\))


Chord with a given middle point

Equation of the chord of parabola \(y^2\) = 4ax whose middle point is (\(x_1, y_1\)) is y - \(y_1\) = \(2a\over y_1\)(x - \(x_1\))
This is reduced to T = \(S_1\) where T = y\(y_1\) = 2a(x + \(x_1\)) & \(S_1\) = \({y_1}^2\) - 4a\(x_1\)


Diameter of Parabola

The locus of the middle points of a system of parallel chords of a parabola is called a Diameter. Equation to the diameter of a parabola is y = \(2a\over m\), where m = slope of parallel chords.
