ELLIPSE

Tangent to the ellipse

(a)   Point form

Equation of tangent to the given ellipse at its point (\(x_1, y_1\)) is \(x{x_1}\over a^2\) + \(y{y_1}\over b^2\) = 1

(b)   Slope form

Equation of tangent to the given ellipse whose slope is 'm', is y = mx \(\pm\) \(\sqrt{a^2m^2 + b^2}\)

Point of contact are (\({\pm} a^2m\over \sqrt{a^2m^2 + b^2}\), \({\pm} b^2\over \sqrt{a^2m^2 + b^2}\))

Note that there are two tangents to the ellipse having the same m, i.e. there are two tangents parallel to any given direction.

(c)   Parametric form

Equation of tangent to the given ellipse at its point (acos\(\theta\), bsin\(\theta\)), is \(xcos\theta\over a\) + \(ysin\theta\over b\) = 1

NOTE :

Point of the intersection of the tangents at the point \(\alpha\) & \(\beta\) is (a\(cos{\alpha+\beta\over 2}\over cos{\alpha -\beta\over 2}\), b\(sin{\alpha+\beta\over 2}\over cos{\alpha-\beta\over 2}\))


Normal to the ellipse : \(x^2\over a^2\) + \(y^2\over b^2\) = 1

(a)   Point form

Equation of normal to the given ellipse at (\(x_1, y_1\)) is \(a^2x\over x_1\) + \(b^2y\over y_1\) = \(a^2-b^2\) = \( a^2e^2\)

(b)   Slope form

Equation of normal to the given ellipse whose slope is 'm', is y = mx \(\mp\) \({(a^2-b^2)m}\over \sqrt{a^2 + b^2m^2}\)

(c)   Parametric form

Equation of normal to the given ellipse at its point (acos\(\theta\), bsin\(\theta\)), is \(axsec\theta \) - \(bycosec\theta \) = \(a^2-b^2\)


Chord of contact

If PA and PB be the tangents from the point P(\(x_1, y_1\)) to the ellipse \(x^2\over a^2\) + \(y^2\over b^2\) = 1

The equation of the chord of contact AB is \(x{x_1}\over a^2\) + \(y{y_1}\over b^2\) = 1 or T = 0.


Pair of tangents

If P(\(x_1, y_1\)) be any point lies outside the ellipse \(x^2\over a^2\) + \(y^2\over b^2\) = 1, and a pair of tangents PA, PB can be drawn to it from P. Then the equation of pair of tangents of PA and PB is \(SS_1\) = \(T^2\)

where S = \(x^2\over a^2\) + \(y^2\over b^2\) - 1, \(S_1\) = \({x_1}^2\over a^2\) + \({y_1}^2\over b^2\) - 1, T = \(x{x_1}\over a^2\) + \(y{y_1}\over b^2\) - 1


Director Circle

Locus of the point of intersection of the tangents which meet at right angles is called the Director circle. The equation to the locus is \(x^2+y^2\) = \(a^2+b^2\) i.e. a circle whose centre is the centre of the ellipse & whose radius is the length of the line joining the ends of the major & minor axis.


Equation of chord with mid point (\(x_1,y_1\))

The equation of the chord of the ellipse \(x^2\over a^2\) + \(y^2\over b^2\) = 1, whose mid point be (\(x_1,y_1\)) is T = \(S_1\)

where \(S_1\) = \({x_1}^2\over a^2\) + \({y_1}^2\over b^2\) - 1, T = \(x{x_1}\over a^2\) + \(y{y_1}\over b^2\) - 1
